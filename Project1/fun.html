Fun Stuff!

Action Figure Collecting and Toy Photography

Though it is the newest of my hobbies, I have grown into collecting and playing with collectable toys! I know, I know, it seems childish, but there is a lot of creativity to be had with simple articulated plastic! I mostly collect Marvel and DC characters (I am especially fond of Spider-man), but I've been trying to branch out into other properties as well, such as the AVP universe, or even video games such as Street Fighter. My favorite part of this hobby thus far has been appreciating the sculptwork and engineering put into each of these toys--some of the figures I have collected have nearly 50 points of articulation (which is a LOT of articulation!). Some of my favorite companies that I have collected from are Kaiyodo Revoltech, who are known for supreme articulation, Medicom Toy Mafex, who are known for great classic sculpts and good articulation, and Jada Toys, a domestic company that makes import-level figures for reasonable prices.

Art, Art, Art!

Since I was in first grade, I have had an affinity towards drawing. I distinctly remember writing a book about spiders for my first grade teacher as a present, and meticulously drawing each and every spider I learned about it the top right corner of each page (with quite faithful representation for a 6-year-old, I might add). Since then, I have maintained drawing as a integral part of who I am, and how I express myself. I make traditional pen-and-pencil art, I make digital art, I make pixel art, I make comic-book inspired art, I even paint. One constant is that I'm always looking for an excuse to put pen to paper and let my creativity flow. Im even working on my first commission right now for one of my friends (and bless him, he's been very VERY patient with me as I've continously delayed finishing the piece). Who knows--maybe I'll make art my career one day. I guess we'll just have to wait and see.
